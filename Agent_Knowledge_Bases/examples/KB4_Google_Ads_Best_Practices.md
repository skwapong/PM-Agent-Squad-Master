# KNOWLEDGE BASE 4: GOOGLE ADS BEST PRACTICES

## PLATFORM OVERVIEW
Google Ads is the world's largest search advertising platform, reaching 90%+ of internet users worldwide. The platform includes Search, Display, Video (YouTube), Shopping, and App campaigns. Google processes 8.5 billion searches per day, making it the most powerful intent-based advertising platform.

## GOOGLE ADS CAMPAIGN TYPES

### Search Campaigns
**Overview:** Text ads appearing on Google Search results and search partner sites
**Best for:** High-intent audiences actively searching for products/services
**Ad formats:** Responsive Search Ads (RSAs), Call-only ads
**Targeting:** Keywords, audiences, demographics, locations, devices
**Bidding:** CPC, Target CPA, Target ROAS, Maximize Conversions

**When to Use:**
- Direct response and lead generation
- Capturing active demand
- Brand searches and competitor targeting
- Service-based businesses
- High consideration purchases

### Display Campaigns
**Overview:** Visual banner ads across 2+ million websites and apps in Google Display Network
**Best for:** Brand awareness, retargeting, reaching broad audiences
**Ad formats:** Responsive Display Ads, uploaded image ads (various sizes)
**Targeting:** Demographics, interests, topics, placements, keywords, audiences
**Bidding:** CPM, CPC, Target CPA, Maximize Conversions

**When to Use:**
- Building awareness and consideration
- Retargeting website visitors
- Visual storytelling
- Reaching users earlier in purchase journey
- Expanding reach beyond search

### Video Campaigns (YouTube)
**Overview:** Video ads on YouTube and Google Video Partners
**Best for:** Brand building, product demonstrations, reaching massive audiences
**Ad formats:** Skippable in-stream, Non-skippable, Bumper ads (6s), Video Discovery
**Targeting:** Demographics, interests, topics, placements, keywords, audiences
**Bidding:** CPV, CPM, Target CPA

**When to Use:**
- Brand awareness and storytelling
- Product demonstrations and tutorials
- Reaching younger demographics
- Building emotional connections
- Launching new products

### Shopping Campaigns
**Overview:** Product listings with images, prices, and merchant info on Google Shopping tab and Search
**Best for:** E-commerce, product-based businesses
**Requirements:** Google Merchant Center account, product feed
**Ad formats:** Product Shopping ads, Showcase Shopping ads
**Targeting:** Automatic based on product feed data
**Bidding:** Manual CPC, Enhanced CPC, Target ROAS, Maximize Conversion Value

**When to Use:**
- E-commerce sales
- Products with strong visual appeal
- Competitive pricing scenarios
- Broad product catalogs

### Performance Max Campaigns
**Overview:** Automated campaign type accessing all Google inventory (Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover)
**Best for:** Driving conversions across entire Google ecosystem
**Requirements:** Conversion tracking, quality assets, clear conversion goals
**Targeting:** Automated based on conversion goals and asset groups
**Bidding:** Target CPA or Target ROAS (required)

**When to Use:**
- Maximizing conversions across all channels
- Accounts with sufficient conversion data (50+ conversions/month)
- Limited time for campaign management
- Supplementing existing Search campaigns

### App Campaigns
**Overview:** Promote mobile apps across Search, Display, YouTube, and Google Play
**Best for:** App installs and in-app actions
**Targeting:** Automated based on app info and creative assets
**Bidding:** Target CPI (cost per install), Target CPA for in-app actions

## GOOGLE SEARCH CAMPAIGNS - DEEP DIVE

### Keyword Research & Strategy
**Keyword Match Types:**

**Broad Match:**
- Shows ads for related searches including synonyms, misspellings, related intents
- Maximum reach, less control
- Example: "women's shoes" may show for "ladies sneakers," "female boots"
- Best with Smart Bidding and healthy conversion data
- Use sparingly with careful negative keyword management

**Phrase Match:**
- Shows ads for searches containing the meaning of keyword
- More control than broad, more reach than exact
- Example: "moving services" shows for "affordable moving services," "interstate moving services"
- Balanced approach for most advertisers

**Exact Match:**
- Shows ads for searches with same meaning or intent
- Maximum control, limited reach
- Example: [moving services] shows for "moving services," "services for moving"
- Best for high-converting, known terms and brand names

**Negative Keywords:**
- Prevents ads from showing for irrelevant searches
- Critical for controlling costs and improving Quality Score
- Add at campaign or ad group level
- Regular review of search terms report essential
- Build comprehensive negative keyword lists by theme

**Keyword Organization:**
- Single Keyword Ad Groups (SKAGs) for tight control and relevance
- Theme-based ad groups (5-20 related keywords) for efficiency
- Separate ad groups for different match types of same keyword
- Separate campaigns for brand vs. non-brand terms
- Include modifiers: buy, cheap, best, reviews, near me, 2025

### Responsive Search Ads (RSAs)
**Structure:**
- 3-15 headlines (15 max, 3 shown at time)
- 2-4 descriptions (4 max, 2 shown at time)
- Google automatically tests combinations
- Pin important elements to specific positions if needed

**Headline Best Practices:**
- Include primary keyword in 2-3 headlines
- Highlight unique selling propositions
- Include calls-to-action
- Add numbers and specifics (discounts, timelines, quantities)
- Use emotional triggers
- Mention brand name
- Character limit: 30 characters per headline

**Description Best Practices:**
- Expand on benefits and features
- Include secondary keywords
- Strong call-to-action
- Address pain points
- Mention promotions or guarantees
- Character limit: 90 characters per description

**Ad Strength:**
- Aim for "Excellent" rating
- Provide maximum unique headlines and descriptions
- Vary content (don't repeat same message)
- Use all available ad extensions

### Ad Extensions (Assets)
**Sitelink Extensions:**
- Additional links below main ad
- 2-6 sitelinks shown
- Use specific landing pages (not homepage)
- Examples: "Free Shipping," "Size Guide," "Reviews," "Contact Us"

**Callout Extensions:**
- Short snippets of additional info (25 characters)
- Examples: "24/7 Support," "Free Returns," "Price Match Guarantee"
- Add 4-6 callouts minimum

**Structured Snippet Extensions:**
- Predetermined categories of products/services
- Examples: Brands, Courses, Destinations, Service Catalog
- Showcase variety and options

**Call Extensions:**
- Display phone number with ad
- Enable click-to-call on mobile
- Track phone call conversions
- Set call hours to match business hours

**Location Extensions:**
- Show business address and map
- Drive foot traffic to physical locations
- Connect Google Business Profile

**Price Extensions:**
- Show product/service prices
- Up to 8 cards with prices
- Direct links to specific offerings

**Promotion Extensions:**
- Highlight sales and special offers
- Include occasion (Black Friday, New Year)
- Add promo codes

**Image Extensions:**
- Visual assets alongside text ads
- Square (1:1) images
- Add visual appeal to search ads

### Quality Score Optimization
**Quality Score Components:**
1. Expected CTR (40% weight): Historical ad click performance
2. Ad Relevance (40% weight): How closely ad matches search intent
3. Landing Page Experience (20% weight): Page relevance, load time, mobile-friendliness

**Improvement Strategies:**
- Create tightly themed ad groups
- Write highly relevant ad copy matching keywords
- Include keywords in headlines and descriptions
- Ensure landing page directly addresses search query
- Improve landing page speed (under 3 seconds)
- Mobile-optimize all landing pages
- Clear, prominent call-to-action on landing page
- Maintain high CTR through compelling ads

**Quality Score Benefits:**
- Lower cost-per-click
- Better ad positions
- Higher impression share
- Improved ROI

## GOOGLE DISPLAY CAMPAIGNS

### Responsive Display Ads
**Required Assets:**
- Images: Up to 15 (landscape 1.91:1, square 1:1)
- Logos: Up to 5
- Headlines: Up to 5 (30 character limit)
- Long Headlines: 1 (90 character limit)
- Descriptions: Up to 5 (90 character limit)
- Business name

**Image Specifications:**
- Landscape: 1200 x 628 pixels minimum
- Square: 1200 x 1200 pixels minimum
- File size: Under 5MB
- Format: PNG, JPG, GIF

**Best Practices:**
- Provide maximum number of assets
- Use high-quality, relevant images
- Avoid text-heavy images
- Include logo variations
- Test different messaging angles

### Display Targeting Options
**Demographic Targeting:**
- Age, gender, parental status, household income
- More granular than Search campaigns

**Affinity Audiences:**
- Users with sustained interest in topics
- Examples: Food & Dining, Sports Fans, Travel Buffs
- Best for awareness and consideration

**In-Market Audiences:**
- Actively researching or comparing products/services
- Examples: Auto buyers, Home improvement shoppers
- Best for driving conversions

**Custom Intent Audiences:**
- Build audiences based on keywords they search
- Layer search behavior onto Display targeting
- High-intent Display targeting

**Custom Affinity Audiences:**
- Create audiences based on interests, URLs, apps
- More specific than pre-built affinity audiences

**Remarketing:**
- Website visitors (standard remarketing)
- Dynamic remarketing (show products viewed)
- Video remarketers (YouTube engagers)
- Customer Match (email lists)

**Similar Audiences:**
- Users similar to remarketing lists or customer lists
- Expansion strategy for proven audiences

**Content Targeting:**
- Topics: Broad content categories
- Placements: Specific websites/apps/videos/channels
- Keywords: Show ads on pages about specific topics
- Exclude placements: Prevent ads on unsuitable content

## GOOGLE VIDEO CAMPAIGNS (YOUTUBE)

### YouTube Ad Formats
**Skippable In-Stream Ads:**
- Can skip after 5 seconds
- 12 seconds - 6 minutes length
- Charged when viewer watches 30 seconds or interacts
- Best for: Storytelling, product demos, consideration
- Optimize first 5 seconds for maximum impact

**Non-Skippable In-Stream Ads:**
- Must watch entire ad (15-20 seconds)
- Charged per 1,000 impressions (CPM)
- Best for: Brand awareness, concise messages
- Higher completion rate, higher cost

**Bumper Ads:**
- 6 seconds, non-skippable
- Charged per 1,000 impressions (CPM)
- Best for: Brand recall, complementing longer campaigns
- Concise, memorable messaging essential

**Video Discovery Ads:**
- Thumbnail image with text
- Appears in YouTube search results, related videos, mobile homepage
- Charged when user clicks to watch
- Best for: Driving channel engagement, reaching searchers

**YouTube Shorts Ads:**
- Vertical video ads (9:16)
- Up to 60 seconds
- Appears between Shorts
- Best for: Reaching younger audiences, mobile-first approach

### YouTube Targeting
**Demographics:** Age, gender, parental status, household income
**Interests:** Affinity, In-Market, Life Events
**Keywords:** Show ads around videos related to keywords
**Topics:** Target content categories
**Placements:** Specific channels or videos
**Audiences:** Remarketing, Customer Match, Similar Audiences

### Video Creative Best Practices
- Hook viewers in first 5 seconds
- Show product/brand early (within 5 seconds)
- Include clear call-to-action
- Add captions (80% watch without sound)
- Optimize for mobile viewing
- Use vertical or square formats for mobile placements
- Include end screen with CTA
- Test different story structures

## GOOGLE SHOPPING CAMPAIGNS

### Google Merchant Center Setup
**Product Feed Requirements:**
- Product ID, Title, Description, Link, Image link
- Price, Availability, Brand, GTIN/MPN
- Product category (Google product taxonomy)
- Condition (new, refurbished, used)

**Feed Optimization:**
- Titles: Include brand, product type, key attributes, color, size (150 characters)
- Descriptions: Detailed, keyword-rich (5,000 characters max)
- Images: High quality, white or lifestyle backgrounds (minimum 100 x 100 pixels, recommended 800+)
- Accurate pricing and availability (synced real-time)
- Complete all optional attributes (size, color, material, pattern)

### Shopping Campaign Structure
**Campaign Priority:**
- Low, Medium, High priority settings
- Control which campaign serves for overlapping products
- Strategic bidding by priority level

**Product Groups:**
- All Products (default)
- Segment by: Category, Brand, Item ID, Product Type, Condition, Custom Labels
- Create granular structure for bid control
- Bid higher on best performers

**Custom Labels:**
- 5 custom labels available (0-4)
- Use for: Margin, seasonality, performance tier, promotion
- Example: custom_label_0 = "high_margin", custom_label_1 = "holiday_2025"

### Shopping Campaign Optimization
- Review search terms and add negatives
- Optimize product titles with high-performing search terms
- Test different image styles (white background vs. lifestyle)
- Adjust bids by device, location, time
- Create separate campaigns for brand vs. generic searches
- Use promotion extensions for sales
- Monitor competitor pricing
- Optimize for Target ROAS based on product margins

## GOOGLE ADS BIDDING STRATEGIES

### Manual Bidding
**Manual CPC:**
- Set maximum CPC bids yourself
- Full control over costs
- Best for: New accounts, testing, limited budgets
- Time-intensive, requires frequent monitoring

### Automated Bidding (Smart Bidding)
**Target CPA:**
- Automatically sets bids to achieve target cost-per-acquisition
- Requires conversion tracking
- Best for: Consistent conversion costs across campaigns
- Minimum: 30 conversions in last 30 days

**Target ROAS:**
- Optimize for specific return on ad spend percentage
- Requires conversion value tracking
- Best for: E-commerce, varied product values
- Minimum: 50 conversions in last 30 days

**Maximize Conversions:**
- Get most conversions within budget
- No CPA target set
- Best for: Building conversion volume, limited historical data
- Can lead to high costs without constraints

**Maximize Conversion Value:**
- Optimize for highest total conversion value
- Best for: E-commerce, maximizing revenue
- Similar to Max Conversions but value-focused

**Target Impression Share:**
- Show in specific percentage of auctions
- Choose location: Anywhere, Top of page, Absolute top
- Best for: Brand awareness, competitive positioning
- Can be expensive for competitive terms

### Bid Adjustments
- Device: Mobile, tablet, desktop (-100% to +900%)
- Location: Increase/decrease bids by geography
- Time of day: Day parting based on performance
- Audience: Layer audience targeting with bid modifiers
- Not available with all automated strategies

## GOOGLE ADS MEASUREMENT & TRACKING

### Conversion Tracking
**Google Ads Conversion Tag:**
- Install on conversion pages (thank you, purchase confirmation)
- Track multiple conversion actions
- Assign values to conversions
- Set conversion windows (1-90 days)

**Conversion Actions:**
- Website conversions: Purchases, sign-ups, form submissions, phone calls
- App conversions: Installs, in-app actions
- Import from Google Analytics 4
- Offline conversions: Upload CRM data

**Attribution Models:**
- Last Click: Full credit to last ad clicked
- First Click: Full credit to first ad clicked
- Linear: Equal credit across touchpoints
- Time Decay: More credit to recent interactions
- Position-Based: 40% first, 40% last, 20% middle
- Data-Driven: Machine learning-based (recommended, requires sufficient data)

### Google Analytics 4 Integration
- Link Google Ads and GA4 accounts
- Import GA4 goals as conversion actions
- Access deeper audience insights
- Analyze full customer journey
- Create remarketing audiences from GA4
- Use GA4 audiences in Google Ads
