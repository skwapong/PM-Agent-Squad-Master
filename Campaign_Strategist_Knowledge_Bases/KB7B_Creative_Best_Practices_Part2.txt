# KNOWLEDGE BASE 7B: CREATIVE BEST PRACTICES & AD FORMATS (PART 2 OF 2)

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC CREATIVE GUIDELINES

### META (Facebook & Instagram)
**Feed Placement:**
- Image: 1080 x 1080 (1:1) or 1080 x 1350 (4:5)
- Video: 1:1 or 4:5, 15-60 seconds
- Carousel: 1080 x 1080 per card, 2-10 cards
- Text: Primary text 125 characters (125-150 max visible), headline 40 characters
- Design: Mobile-first, show product/brand early, high-quality visuals

**Stories:**
- Image/Video: 1080 x 1920 (9:16)
- Video: 6-15 seconds
- Text: Large, upper/lower thirds (avoid center - covered by profile pic)
- Design: Vertical full-screen, interactive elements, authentic/raw aesthetic
- No clickable links in organic Stories (swipe-up for ads)

**Reels:**
- Video: 1080 x 1920 (9:16)
- Length: 15-60 seconds (15-30 ideal)
- Style: Entertaining, trend-driven, music-focused, fast-paced
- Text: Minimal, large font
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds

**Carousel Best Practices:**
- First card most important (highest viewership)
- Tell story across cards or show product variations
- Each card can have unique link
- Use final card for strong CTA
- Algorithm auto-arranges by performance

### PINTEREST
**Standard Pins:**
- Image: 1000 x 1500 (2:3 vertical)
- High-quality, bright, aspirational imagery
- Text overlay with description/benefit
- Lifestyle context preferred over white background
- Pin title: 100 characters
- Pin description: 500 characters with keywords

**Video Pins:**
- Video: 1000 x 1500 (2:3) or 1:1
- Length: 6-15 seconds for ads
- Cover image crucial (first frame or custom)
- Captions required
- How-to and tutorial content performs well

**Carousel Pins:**
- 2-5 images, 1:1 or 2:3
- Each image should standalone
- Use for product collections or step-by-step

**Creative Considerations:**
- Vertical orientation essential
- Bright, saturated colors outperform muted
- Show product in inspiring context
- Include descriptive text on image
- Avoid dark or cluttered images
- Aspirational over realistic

### GOOGLE ADS
**Search Ads (Text Only):**
- Headlines: 3-15 headlines, 30 characters each
- Descriptions: 2-4 descriptions, 90 characters each
- Include keywords in headlines
- Highlight unique value propositions
- Clear call-to-action
- Use all extensions/assets available

**Display Ads (Responsive):**
- Images: Landscape (1200 x 628) and Square (1200 x 1200)
- Logos: 1200 x 1200 (square) and 1200 x 300 (landscape)
- Headlines: 5 short (30 char) + 1 long (90 char)
- Descriptions: 5 descriptions (90 char each)
- Clean, professional design
- Clear value proposition
- Consistent branding

**YouTube Ads:**
- In-Stream Skippable: 12 seconds - 6 minutes (optimize for 15-30s)
- Non-Skippable: 15-20 seconds
- Bumper: Exactly 6 seconds
- Hook in first 5 seconds (before skip)
- Show brand early
- Include captions
- End screen with CTA
- Clickable CTAs and overlays

### TIKTOK
**In-Feed Ads:**
- Video: 1080 x 1920 (9:16)
- Length: 9-15 seconds (up to 60 allowed)
- Authentic, UGC-style content
- Fast-paced editing
- Trending sounds and effects
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds
- Native, non-promotional look

**Creative Requirements:**
- Vertical full-screen format
- Sound-on optimization (music/voiceover essential)
- Quick cuts and transitions
- Text overlays in upper 2/3
- Entertaining first, selling second
- Participate in trends creatively

## CREATIVE OPTIMIZATION & TESTING

### Performance Analysis
**Creative Metrics to Monitor:**
- CTR: Is creative engaging enough to click?
- Video view rate: Is hook compelling enough?
- Engagement rate: Is content resonating?
- Conversion rate: Does creative drive action?
- Cost per result: Is creative efficient?
- Frequency: Is creative fatiguing?

**Creative Fatigue Indicators:**
- CTR declining >20%
- Rising CPC/CPA
- Frequency >5
- Declining engagement rate
- Audience feedback (negative comments)

**Refresh Strategies:**
- Proactive refresh every 2-4 weeks
- Reactive refresh when fatigue indicators appear
- Seasonal refreshes (holiday themes, weather, events)
- Promotional refreshes (sales, new products, limited offers)
- Test before replacing entirely

### A/B Testing Framework
**Image Testing:**
- Lifestyle vs. product shot
- Different color schemes
- Different focal points
- With/without people
- Different product angles

**Video Testing:**
- Different hooks (first 3 seconds)
- Different lengths
- Different formats (testimonial vs. demo)
- Music vs. voiceover vs. silent with captions

**Copy Testing:**
- Benefit-focused vs. feature-focused
- Different value propositions
- Long-form vs. short-form
- Different tones (urgent vs. educational)
- Different CTAs

**Format Testing:**
- Image vs. video
- Single image vs. carousel
- Square vs. vertical vs. horizontal

**Statistical Significance:**
- Minimum 100 clicks or 50 conversions per variation
- Run for at least 7 days
- 95% confidence level
- Use platform testing tools (Meta A/B Test, Google Experiments)

### Creative Personalization
**Dynamic Creative:**
- Meta: Automatically tests combinations of assets
- Google: Responsive ads mix headlines/descriptions
- Provide maximum variations for algorithm testing

**Audience-Specific Creative:**
- Different messaging for different personas
- Age-appropriate imagery and language
- Location-specific offers or imagery
- Lifecycle stage appropriate messaging (new vs. returning)

**Seasonal & Timely Creative:**
- Holiday themes (45-60 days advance planning)
- Seasonal relevance (weather, events)
- Current trends and cultural moments
- Time-sensitive offers

## CREATIVE PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### In-House vs. Agency
**In-House Advantages:**
- Brand knowledge and authenticity
- Faster iteration and testing
- Lower cost per asset
- Closer collaboration with marketing team

**Agency/Freelancer Advantages:**
- Professional expertise and equipment
- Fresh perspective
- Scalability for large projects
- Specialized skills (animation, video production)

**Hybrid Approach:**
- Agency for hero/campaign creative
- In-house for iterations and testing
- Freelancers for specialized needs
- User-generated content for authenticity

### Creative Tools & Resources
**Design Tools:**
- Canva: Templates, easy design, team collaboration
- Adobe Creative Suite: Professional design (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere)
- Figma: Collaborative interface design

**Video Editing:**
- CapCut: Mobile-friendly, trending effects
- Adobe Premiere Pro: Professional video editing
- Final Cut Pro: Mac-based professional editing
- iMovie: Basic, user-friendly

**Stock Resources:**
- Unsplash, Pexels: Free stock photos
- Shutterstock, Adobe Stock: Premium stock photos and videos
- Artlist, Epidemic Sound: Royalty-free music
- Canva: Stock photos, videos, graphics within platform

**AI Creative Tools:**
- Midjourney, DALL-E: AI image generation
- Runway: AI video editing and generation
- Copy.ai, Jasper: AI copywriting assistance
- Use as starting point, not final product

### Creative Asset Organization
**Naming Conventions:**
- Platform_Campaign_Format_Version_Date
- Example: Meta_HolidaySale_Video_V2_2025-12-01

**Asset Library Management:**
- Centralized storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, DAM)
- Folder structure by campaign/platform/format
- Version control
- Archive after campaign completion
- Document performance notes

## CREATIVE BEST PRACTICES SUMMARY

### Universal Creative Principles
**Always:**
- Mobile-first design and testing
- Clear value proposition in first 3 seconds
- High-quality, professional visuals
- Strong, specific call-to-action
- Brand consistency across all assets
- Test multiple variations
- Refresh creative regularly
- Monitor performance metrics closely

**Avoid:**
- Overly promotional or salesy tone
- Poor quality images or video
- Unclear or missing CTAs
- Recycling content across platforms without adaptation
- Ignoring platform-specific best practices
- Running same creative beyond fatigue point
- Skipping mobile optimization

### Platform Adaptation Strategy
**Same Campaign, Different Creative:**
- Core message consistent across platforms
- Format adapted to platform (vertical for TikTok/Stories, square for Feed)
- Tone adjusted to platform culture (more polished for Pinterest, raw for TikTok)
- Length optimized for platform (shorter for TikTok, longer for YouTube)
- Platform-specific features utilized (polls, stickers, interactive elements)

### Creative Testing Roadmap
**Week 1-2: Initial Launch**
- 3-5 creative variations
- Equal budget distribution
- Full platform feature set

**Week 2-3: Analysis**
- Identify top performers (CTR, CVR, CPA)
- Analyze what's working (hook, visual style, messaging)
- Document learnings

**Week 3-4: Iteration**
- Create new variations based on insights
- Scale winning elements
- Test new angles on underperformers

**Week 4+: Refresh**
- Introduce completely new creative
- Maintain successful elements
- Prevent audience fatigue

### Creative Quality Checklist
Before launching any creative asset, verify:
□ Correct dimensions for platform and placement
□ High resolution and quality
□ Mobile-optimized (readable text, visible elements)
□ Clear brand presence (logo, colors, fonts)
□ Compelling hook (first 3 seconds for video, visual hierarchy for image)
□ Clear value proposition
□ Strong, specific call-to-action
□ Captions on video (if applicable)
□ No spelling or grammar errors
□ Complies with platform policies
□ Aligns with brand guidelines
□ Tracking parameters added to URLs
□ A/B test variant prepared (if testing)
